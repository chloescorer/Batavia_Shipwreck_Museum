FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

At the Batavia exhibit, visitors can witness an impressive collection of artifacts salvaged from the shipwreck, including cannons, navigational tools, personal belongings, and sections of the original hull. The exhibit offers a captivating glimpse into the tragic history of the Batavia and its ill-fated voyage, immersing visitors in a rich narrative of maritime exploration and survival.
We are open daily from 9:30am to 5pm, providing ample opportunities for visitors to explore the captivating artifacts and immerse themselves in the tragic history of the shipwreck. Please note that the exhibit has special opening hours on ANZAC Day from 1:00pm to 5:00pm and remains closed on Christmas Day, Boxing Day, New Year's Day, and Good Friday.
The entry to the Batavia exhibit at the Fremantle Shipwreck Museum is free for visitors, with a suggested donation of $5 to support the preservation and maintenance of this historically significant site.
The Batavia exhibit is suitable for children as it offers a dedicated play area for them to enjoy. However, parents should be aware that certain parts of the exhibit may contain slightly scary elements that could be unsettling for younger children.
The Batavia Gallery is completely accessible to people with disabilities. It features automatic door buttons to assist with opening heavy doors, and elevators are available to ensure convenient access to the upper levels of the exhibit.
Visitors are allowed to take photographs at the Batavia gallery. However, to preserve the delicate artifacts, flash photography is not permitted to ensure their long-term preservation and protection.
Yes you can, visitors can find a delightful selection of souvenirs available for purchase at the small gift shop conveniently located to the left of the entry to the Batavia gallery.
